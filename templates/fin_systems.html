Fin Systems Guide

Understanding Fin Box Systems

Fin box systems are the mechanisms that connect fins to your surfboard. Different systems have unique characteristics, affecting how fins perform and how easily they can be changed.

FCS (Fin Control System)

The original removable fin system, FCS uses two plugs per fin with tabs that insert into the plugs.

Variations:

  • FCS I - The original system (being phased out)
  • FCS II - Tool-less fin installation with improved connection
  • FCS Fusion - Molded directly into surfboard foam

Pros:

  • Widely available fins
  • FCS II allows quick changes without tools
  • Slightly more flex than Futures

Cons:

  • FCS I fins can pop out in powerful waves
  • Two plugs per fin create more potential failure points

Futures Fins

Futures uses a single, longer box per fin with a solid base and screw lock system.

Characteristics:

  • Single box per fin
  • Requires hex key for installation
  • Solid connection from base to tip

Pros:

  • Very secure connection
  • Less prone to breakage
  • More direct energy transfer

Cons:

  • Requires tool for installation/removal
  • Slightly less flex than FCS
  • Can be harder to repair if damaged

Longboard Fin Systems

Longboards often use different fin systems than shortboards.

Common Types:

  • Single Fin Box - Traditional longboard setup
  • 2+1 Setup - Center fin box with FCS/Futures side fins
  • Pivot Fin Box - Allows fin rotation for traditional longboarding
  • Tuttle Box - Deep box used for larger center fins

Other Fin Systems

Less common but still found in the market.

Systems:

  • LokBox - Similar to Futures but with different locking mechanism
  • ProBox - Used on some older boards
  • Glass-On Fins - Permanently attached fins (no box system)
  • Soft Board Fin Systems - Used on foam boards with unique attachment methods

Fin System Compatibility

Understanding fin system compatibility is crucial when buying new fins or boards.

Fin System Compatible With Not Compatible With
FCS I FCS I fins FCS II, Futures, other systems
FCS II FCS II fins, FCS I fins (with less security) Futures, other systems
Futures Futures fins only FCS I, FCS II, other systems
